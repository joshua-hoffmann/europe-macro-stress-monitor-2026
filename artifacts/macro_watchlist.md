# Macro Watchlist
## Europe Macro Stress Monitor 2026

## Purpose

This artifact lists the indicators that should be monitored to observe Europe’s 2026 macro stress profile.

The watchlist is not a forecasting model. It does not produce automatic warnings, rankings, investment signals or policy recommendations.

## Watchlist

| Field | Indicator | Geography | Main sources | Suggested update rhythm | Why it matters | Claim boundary |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Real GDP growth | Euro area, Austria | OECD, European Commission, OeNB | Quarterly or when projections update | Shows macro buffer and recovery strength | No recession forecast |
| Inflation | HICP / headline inflation | Euro area, Austria | ECB, Eurostat, OeNB | Monthly | Shows visible price pressure | No permanent-inflation claim |
| Core inflation | Inflation excluding energy and food | Euro area, Austria | ECB, Eurostat | Monthly | Shows whether price pressure is broadening | No automatic interest-rate inference |
| Energy | Oil, gas and energy inflation | Europe-relevant, Austria | ECB, European Commission, IEA, Eurostat | Weekly to monthly | Shows external shock channel | No energy-price forecast |
| Investment | Private investment / confidence | Euro area, Austria | OECD, European Commission, Eurostat | Quarterly or monthly depending on indicator | Shows whether uncertainty affects future growth | No company or sector forecast |
| Fiscal space | Deficit, debt ratio, primary balance | EU, euro area, Austria | European Commission, Eurostat | Forecast update cycle | Shows public finance buffer | No debt-crisis claim |
| Labour markets | Unemployment and employment | Euro area, Austria | Eurostat, OECD, national sources | Monthly | Shows income and consumption resilience | No individual job-risk claim |
| Credit conditions | Lending standards, credit costs, bond yields | Euro area, Austria | ECB, market/institutional sources | Quarterly or monthly | Shows financing pressure | No credit, asset or investment advice |
| Consumption | Retail sales, real income, savings | Euro area, Austria | Eurostat, OECD, national sources | Monthly to quarterly | Shows household demand strength | No household behaviour forecast |
| Uncertainty | Geopolitics, trade, policy uncertainty | Europe-relevant | OECD, European Commission, IEA | Event-driven | Shows expectation and investment pressure | No geopolitical forecast |

## Suggested status language

The project may later use simple status labels, but only as source-bounded monitoring aids.

Allowed status labels:

- Monitor
- Elevated attention
- Visible stress
- Data gap / unclear

These labels must not be presented as objective risk levels, investment signals, policy triggers or crisis warnings.

## Update logic

A watchlist update should record:

- source,
- date accessed,
- indicator,
- geography,
- period,
- value or qualitative observation,
- safe interpretation,
- claim boundary,
- whether the item changed from the prior update.

## Public interpretation

The watchlist helps readers understand which macro channels deserve attention.

It does not say what will happen next.

## Strongest safe observation

A structured watchlist is the safest and most useful format for this project because it preserves uncertainty while still making Europe’s macro stress profile observable.
