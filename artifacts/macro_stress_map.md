# Macro Stress Map
## Euro Area and Austria

## Purpose

This artifact maps visible macroeconomic pressure points across the euro area and Austria.

It is a source-bounded observation matrix, not a forecast model, country ranking, investment signal or policy recommendation tool.

## Stress map

| Dimension | Indicator | Euro area use | Austria use | Safe interpretation | Claim boundary |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Growth | Real GDP growth | Shows euro area growth buffer | Shows Austria’s growth position within the European context | Weak growth lowers the ability to absorb shocks | Do not forecast recession |
| Inflation | HICP / headline inflation | Shows visible price pressure | Shows Austria’s comparable inflation context | Elevated inflation remains a macro stress factor | Do not claim permanent inflation |
| Core inflation | Inflation excluding energy and food | Shows whether price pressure is broadening | Helps distinguish energy-driven pressure from broader pressure | Core inflation helps identify persistence risk | Do not infer automatic interest-rate decisions |
| Energy | Energy prices / energy inflation | Shows external shock channel | Shows energy sensitivity in the Austria case | Energy links geopolitics, inflation and business costs | Do not forecast oil, gas or electricity prices |
| Investment | Private investment / confidence | Shows whether uncertainty affects real activity | Shows whether Austria’s recovery is investment-supported | Investment hesitation can weaken future growth | Do not forecast individual firms or sectors |
| Fiscal space | Deficit, debt ratio, primary balance | Shows public finance pressure | Shows Austria’s budget-space context | Fiscal space matters more when growth is weak | Do not claim fiscal crisis |
| Labour markets | Unemployment / employment | Shows consumption and income resilience | Shows whether Austria’s labour market buffers stress | Labour resilience can soften stress | Do not infer individual job risk |
| Credit conditions | Lending standards / financing costs | Shows financing pressure | Shows investment and household financing environment | Tighter conditions can weigh on investment and consumption | Do not give credit or asset advice |
| Consumption | Retail, real income, household demand | Shows demand-side resilience | Shows household pressure in Austria | Consumption can buffer or amplify macro stress | Do not forecast household behaviour |
| Uncertainty | Geopolitical, trade and policy uncertainty | Shows external expectation shocks | Shows uncertainty exposure in Austria | Uncertainty affects prices, investment and confidence | Do not forecast geopolitical outcomes |

## Interpretation rule

For every row, the project should separate:

1. what the source says,
2. what dimension the source informs,
3. what the strongest safe interpretation is,
4. what must not be claimed.

## Allowed use

The map may be used to explain the structure of Europe’s macro stress profile.

It may be used to compare visible pressure dimensions between the euro area and Austria.

## Forbidden use

The map must not be used as:

- a recession forecast,
- a country-risk ranking,
- a fiscal crisis signal,
- an investment signal,
- a policy recommendation,
- a complete early-warning model.

## Strongest safe observation

The macro stress map shows that Europe’s 2026 fragility is best understood as a combination of connected pressures rather than as one single crisis signal.
